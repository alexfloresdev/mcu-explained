The Marvel Cinematic Universe (often referred to as the MCU) is comprised of 20 interconnected movies (with more on the way), organized into three separate "phases," centered on the stories of over 20 different comic book characters (with more on the way) who originate in the pages of Marvel Comics

While other movies have been made by different movie studios based on comic book characters who also originate in Marvel Comics, the MCU has thus far been the most successful attempt at creating a series of interconnected movies that both tell the individual stories of their characters while simultaneously connecting those stories to a larger narrative that expands across multiple movies

The 15+ movies of the MCU are made by the Disney owned company, Marvel Studios. Marvel Studios is led by current president and all around awesome guy, Kevin Feige

Phase 01

Major MCU Events

  • Tony Stark aka Iron Man is introduced into the MCU
  • Happy Hogan is introduced into the MCU
  • Pepper Potts is introduced into the MCU
  • JARVIS is introduced into the MCU
  • Colonel James Rhodes is introduced into the MCU (originally played by Terrence Howard)
  • After a bomb goes off near Tony Stark, an electromagnet is implanted in Tony's chest to prevent shrapnel from reaching his heart
  • Tony Stark invents the arc reactor technology which powers all of his Iron Man suits and also serves as an upgraded electromagnet implanted in his chest
  • Agent Coulson is introduced into the MCU
  • S.H.I.E.L.D. is subtly introduced using their full name of the "Strategic Homeland Intervention, Enforcement and Logistics Division"
  • Seeing how Stark Enterprises, his weapons manufacturing company, is dealing on both sides of the table, Tony uses his Iron Man armor to go behind enemy lines and destroy his weapons
  • Tony Stark announces his identity to the world as "Iron Man"
  • Nick Fury, director of S.H.I.E.L.D. shows up in a post credit sequence to propose an idea of the Avengers Initiative

Major MCU Events

  • Bruce Banner aka The Hulk is introduced into the MCU (originally played by Edward Norton)
  • General Thaddeus "Thunderbolt" Ross is introduced into the MCU
  • In a failed attempt to create a super soldier, Bruce Banner accidentally transforms himself into the Hulk
  • Bruce Banner is unsuccessful in finding a cure for preventing his Hulk transformation
  • When an unstoppable threat hits Harlem, Bruce Banner uses the Hulk to stop said threat and in doing so it's implied that the Hulk might be more than just a mindless vehicle of destruction
  • Many months after the above incident, Bruce Banner is seen meditating trying to control at will his transformation into the Hulk
  • Tony Stark makes a brief cameo to discuss with General Ross to discuss the possibility of the Avengers team

Major MCU Events

  • Tony Stark is praised by the masses as Iron Man and pressured by the government to hand over his Iron Man technology
  • Colonel James Rhodes is re-introduced into the MCU (Now played by Don Cheadle)
  • Natasha Romanoff aka Black Widow is introduced into the MCU
  • Col. Rhodes gets an Iron Man Suit of his own and becomes the War Machine
  • Due to Tony Stark's instability, Nick Fury decides to call off the Avengers initiative
  • Before Tony completely self-destructs, SHIELD steps in to help Tony get back on his feet and upgrade his arc reactor technology
  • A middle-aged Howard Stark - father of Tony Stark - is introduced into the MCU
  • Watching old footage from Howard Stark's promotional videos for Stark Enterpries, Tony Stark discovers a secret message from his father on how to create a new atom
  • Tony Stark creates a new atom to more efficiently and healthily power the arc reactor implanted in his chest and which powers all of his Iron Man suits

Major MCU Events

  • Thor is introduced into the MCU
  • Dr. Erik Selvig is introduced into the MCU
  • Thor's homeworld of Asgard is introduced into the MCU
  • Odin is introduced into the MCU
  • Loki is introduced into the MCU
  • The bifrost - Asgardian technology used to transport individuals across the nine realms - is introduced into the MCU
  • Thor is stripped of his power as god of Thunder and exiled to earth until he can prove himself worthy
  • Clint Barton aka Hawkeye makes his first appearance in the MCU in a brief cameo as a member of S.H.I.E.L.D.
  • Loki discovers he was adopted and his conspiracy plot to take Thor's place as ruler of Asgard is revealed
  • In an attempt to kill Thor while he is still on earth, Loki sends the Destroyer to assasinate Thor
  • In the process of trying to stop the Destroyer, Thor proves himself worthy of the rule of Asgard and wielding his weapon Mjulnir
  • Thor destroys the Bifrost Bridge in order to stop Loki from wiping out a planet of enemies to Asgard
  • Rather than allow Odin and Thor to save Loki and return to Asgard, Loki chooses to drift off into space
  • Loki is next seen on earth in a S.H.I.E.L.D. facility with his eye on the Tesseract

Major MCU Events

  • Steve Rogers aka Captain America is introduced into the MCU
  • Hydra, a fictional division of the WWII Nazis, is introduced into the MCU
  • The Tesseract - which is later discovered to be an Infinity Stone known as the Space Stone - is introduced into the MCU
  • Hydra uses the Tesseract to power new technology and new types of weapons, including bombs they intend to drop over major American cities
  • Bucky Barnes - lifelong childhood friend to Steve Rogers - is introduced into the MCU
  • Dr. Arnim Zola - right-hand man of the Red Skull, leader of Hydra - is introduced into the MCU
  • Agent Peggy Carter is introduced into the MCU
  • A young Howard Stark - future father of Tony Stark - is introduced into the MCU
  • Shortly after Steve Rogers becomes Captain America, he is tasked with leading a small squadron to take down Hydra bases
  • Captain America is given his shield made of a fictional metal known as Vibranium
  • In one of their final missions, Captain America and his squadron are able to capture Dr. Arnim Zola, however Bucky Barnes falls from a great height and is presumed to be dead
  • Informed by Dr. Zola, Captain America and company locate Hydra's final stronghold and arrive just in time to stop Hydra from using a bomber plane to drop bombs over major American cities
  • In the process of stopping the Hydra bomber plane, the Tesseract is lost at sea
  • In order to stop the Hydra bomber plane, Steve Rogers intentionally pilots the plane into the Arctic ocean at the expense of his own life
  • Though the Tesseract is eventually found by Howard Stark, they are unable to find Steve Rogers
  • Steve Rogers wakes up in present day New York City, nearly 70 years after the events of the film have transpired.
  • Shortly after Steve Rogers wakes up, Nick Fury shows up to recruit Steve Rogers for the Avengers

Major MCU Events

  • Clint Barton, aka Hawkeye is officially introduced into the MCU
  • Agent Maria Hill is introduced into the MCU
  • Within a SHIELD facility where the Tesseract is being experimented upon by Dr. Erik Selvig and under the watch of Clint Barton, a portal is opened by the Tesseract from which Loki appears with a scepter in hand
  • Located within the gem of Loki's scepter, the Infinity Stone known as the Mindstone is introduced into the MCU
  • Loki uses his scepter to mentally enslave Hawkeye and Dr. Erik Selvig
  • In opening the portal, the entire SHIELD facility collapes and Loki escapes with the Tesseract
  • Nick Fury reactivates the "Avengers Initiative" sending Natasha Romanoff to recruit Bruce Banner, Agent Phil Coulson to recruit Tony Stark and personally going to recruit Captain America
  • Stark Tower is introduced into the MCU
  • Bruce Banner aka The Hulk is re-introduced into the MCU (now played by Mark Ruffalo)
  • When Captain America and Iron Man are successful in capturing Loki, Thor shows up to take part in questioning Loki and hopefully bring him back to Asgard
  • The SHIELD Helicarrier technology is introduced into the MCU
  • Loki is imprisoned aboard a SHIELD Helicarrier while Bruce Banner works to track down the Tesseract and the remaining Avengers work to discover what Loki's true plan is as well as SHIELD's
  • In the above process it's revealed that SHIELD intends to use the Tesseract to power weapons similar to the weapons used by Hydra in WWII and that Thor's battle against the Destroyer was their reason for trying to create more powerful weapons.
  • Clint Barton, still under mind control, attacks the SHIELD Helicarrier which causes Bruce Banner to lose control and transform into the Hulk creating chaos in the process
  • Loki escapes, Thor
  • Thor uses the Tesseract to return to Asgard along with Loki as a prisoner

  • The Winter Soldier is introduced into the MCU

  • Wakanda is briefly introduced into the MCU as a primary source of Vibranium
  • The Hulkbuster armor is introduced into the MCU
  • Thor receives a vision that Dr. Erik Selvig helps him interpret
  • The Infinity Stone housed within Loki's scepter known as the Mindstone takes center stage in this film and is used to create Wanda's powers as well as the Vision

  • Ant-Man is introduced into the MCU

Phase 03

  • Now Secretary of State, Thaddeus Ross returns to inform the Avengers that they must submit to UN supervision
  • T'challa aka Black Panther is introduced into the MCU
  • T'challa's father and the King of Wakanda, T'chaka, is killed in a terrorist bombing for which Bucky Barnes aka the Winter Soldier is implicated as responsible
  • Believing Bucky is innocent, Captain America and the Falcon break away from the Avengers to protect Bucky
  • Spider-Man is introduced into the MCU
  • Tony Stark recruits Spider-Man to help bring Captain America and friends in
  • In joining with Captain America to stop the real person responsible for the bombing, Ant-Man, Hawkeye, Scarlet Witch, The Falcon and Black Widow also break from the Avengers and become fugitives
  • War Machine is badly injured in a confrontation with Captain America and company
  • Captain America gives up his shield
  • When T'challa learns who is actually responsible for the bombing and his father's death, T'challa decides to provide asylum for Bucky Barnes and Captain America in Wakanda
  • Hiding within Wakanda, Bucky decides to be frozen again until his Hydra programming can be undone by Wakandan scientists
  • The remaining Avengers are currently Vision, Iron Man and War Machine
